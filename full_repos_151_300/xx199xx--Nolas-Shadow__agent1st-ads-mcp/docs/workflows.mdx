## Launch a campaign from scratch

This is the full end-to-end flow for getting a new Meta campaign live. Follow these steps in order.

**Step 1 — Confirm everything is connected**

Call `check_setup` with no inputs. It will tell you your license tier and whether Meta is connected. If anything is missing, fix it before continuing.

**Step 2 — Check your balance**

Call `get_ad_account_info`. Look at the `balance` field. Make sure you have enough to cover at least a few days of your planned daily budget. If the balance is zero, the campaign will not spend.

**Step 3 — Create the campaign**

Call `create_meta_campaign` with your campaign details — name, objective, budget, ad copy, and targeting. The campaign is created in a PAUSED state. You'll get back a `campaign_id` — save it.

**Step 4 — Go live**

Call `enable_meta_campaign` with the `campaign_id` from step 3. The campaign starts spending immediately.

**Step 5 — Check performance after 24 hours**

Call `get_meta_campaign_stats` with `date_preset: "yesterday"`. Look at CTR (above 1% is decent, above 2% is strong) and CPM (lower is better).

**Step 6 — Scale or adjust**

If performance is strong, call `update_meta_campaign_budget` to increase the daily budget. If it's weak, call `pause_meta_campaign` and revise your ad copy or targeting.

## Daily performance review

Run this every morning to manage all active campaigns efficiently.

**Step 1** — Call `list_meta_campaigns` with `status: "ACTIVE"` to get all currently running campaigns.

**Step 2** — For each campaign ID, call `get_meta_campaign_stats` with `date_preset: "yesterday"` to see how it performed.

**Step 3** — Any campaign with CTR below 0.5% or unusually high CPM — call `pause_meta_campaign` to stop the spend.

**Step 4** — Any campaign with CTR above 2% — call `update_meta_campaign_budget` to increase the budget and capture more volume while it's performing.

## Launch the same campaign on both platforms

Use this when you want to run a campaign on Meta and TikTok at the same time. Requires an Operator plan or higher.

**Step 1** — Call `create_meta_campaign` with your campaign details. Save the `campaign_id`.

**Step 2** — Call `enable_meta_campaign` with the Meta campaign ID.

**Step 3** — Call `create_tiktok_campaign` with similar details. Note: TikTok has a $20/day minimum and uses different objective names (`TRAFFIC` instead of `OUTCOME_TRAFFIC`).

**Step 4** — Call `enable_tiktok_campaign` with the TikTok campaign ID.

Both campaigns are now live. After 24–48 hours, compare stats from `get_meta_campaign_stats` and `get_tiktok_campaign_stats` to see which platform is performing better.

## Stop all spending immediately

Use this to pause everything at once — for example, if you've hit your monthly budget or need to halt all activity.

**Step 1** — Call `list_meta_campaigns` with `status: "ACTIVE"` to get all live Meta campaign IDs.

**Step 2** — Call `pause_meta_campaign` for each one.

**Step 3** — Call `list_tiktok_campaigns` to get TikTok campaign IDs.

**Step 4** — Call `pause_tiktok_campaign` for each active one.

All spending stops immediately. No campaigns are deleted — you can re-enable them anytime.

## Scale a winning campaign

When a campaign is performing well and you want to push more budget into it:

**Step 1** — Call `get_meta_campaign_stats` with `date_preset: "last_7d"` to confirm the performance trend is consistent, not just a one-day spike.

**Step 2** — If CTR is strong and CPM is acceptable across the full 7 days, call `update_meta_campaign_budget` to increase the daily budget. A common approach is to increase by 20–30% at a time rather than doubling overnight — large budget jumps can reset Meta's delivery algorithm.

**Step 3** — Monitor again after 48 hours with another stats call to confirm performance held after the budget increase.
